About WDR5-related disorder

A plain-English guide for families, relatives, professionals and researchers.

What is WDR5?

WDR5 is a gene involved in normal development. Changes in this gene have been linked with a rare neurodevelopmental disorder. Because it is newly described and very rare, many families find there is very little information available.

This website shares family experience and signposts research. It is not medical advice. Always speak to your child’s doctor or genetics team.

Commonly reported features

Families may experience different things. This list should be treated as a starting point, not a complete medical description.

  • Developmental delay or learning differences
  • Speech and communication delay
  • Autistic features or autism diagnosis
  • Seizures or epilepsy in some children
  • Feeding, sleep or behavioural challenges

Current research

The condition is rare and research is still developing. We link to published studies and hope to help connect families who may wish to participate in future research.
